Alfredo Morales

Alfredo Morales is an aspiring software engineer and computer science student at Hunter College.

He is of Hispanic descent and as a teenager lived 5 years in Oaxaca, Mexico. However, growing up in Brooklyn, New York he realized that as time goes on, generations stay at home, even avoiding their next door neighbor. Even a person's motherland is strange to them nowadays. Even though we are more connected than ever, we live contained in a bubble. It is more than evident that due to the current pandemic, communities are less likely to interact, however, by taking a look into how New Yorkers interact with the burst of technology in modern life, we can begin to see patterns.

His work explores the lack or abundance of communities in our lives by observing everyday life and by containing it in photos for everyone to observe and draw their own conclusions.

AN UNEXPLORED LIFE IS NOT WORTH LIVING.

Alfredo is a true believer that an unexplored life is not worth living and has subscribed to the idea that creating art is a very noble way to pursue that purpose.
