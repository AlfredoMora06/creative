Triad Trechet Tiles

Chilly Mood Beginner's Dice

By Alfredo Morales

For this assignment I decided to go for a simple feel. I definitely had inspiration from lecture on the Trechet Tiles. However, although the black and white definitely has something going for it in terms of aesthetic and fanciness, I knew I wanted to add more color even if it is not something you would usually think of when you think of these colored designs. I wanted to give it a very child-like design and pick fun colors. The triadic color scheme from the Adobe color wheel gave me a brighter color that I wanted to use on the squares. I was decided on using darker colors for the main squares and a lighter color with white to have a broader range. This pattern would most likely be used on gift wrapping or even as hypnotic art. If you squint your eyes as you look the pattern, you can see the squares change in sizes. Pretty eerie.

For the second pattern, I wanted to go for a chill-y mood. Imagining a dice, I decided to draw one up from a hexagon, using the line tools I was able to give it a cube look and make it 3-d-ish. The first colors that comes to mind when I think of chill are the colors blue, black and white. Therefore, I decided to experiment with the colors on the dice, keeping the dots and borders black for noticeability and adding more border width on the borders to give a cartoonish feel. I also decided to make the background a lighter blue to not stay in plain white. For it to stand out in its own way I decided to use a darker blue for the dice. Even though this was a beginner trying out new styles I believe this pattern would definitely be out there for some wrapping paper for a kid's birthday or maybe a simple background screen saver, one of those that come as a default.
